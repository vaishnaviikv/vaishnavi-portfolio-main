Tested Intelligent Systems For Traffic Operations (TIESTO)

  • Tech Stack: Embedded C (for Arduino), Gazebo Simulator (ROS-based) for large-scale traffic simulation, C++ (for real-time traffic control logic), RFID, Wireless V2I (Vehicle-to-Infrastructure) Communication
  • Sangam, NIT Trichy: Hackathon Submission

The project "TIESTO: A Self-Adaptive Traffic Control with Wireless V2I Communication" aimed to create an intelligent traffic management system capable of dynamically adjusting traffic signals based on real-time traffic density and vehicle prioritization, particularly for emergency service vehicles (ESVs).

The system utilized RFID technology to detect vehicles at intersections and determine traffic volume. It also employed a smart switching algorithm to reduce congestion, minimize fuel consumption, and decrease pollution.

The Arduino Mega 2560 controlled the traffic lights and processed inputs from RFID detectors, while Gazebo simulation was used for large-scale testing.

It went on to be published in the 2019 International Conference on Advances in Computing, Communication, and Control (ICAC3). This recognition by IEEE highlights the project's innovative approach in reducing vehicle idling time, curbing pollution, and prioritizing emergency service vehicles through intelligent traffic management.
